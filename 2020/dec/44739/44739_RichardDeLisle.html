Dear ##OptionalParameter1##,

As the year draws to a close, I want to thank you for your continued interest and support in the Slavic languages and cultures program. Our students need your help now more than ever. We would like to give you the opportunity to help by naming undergrad fellowships that could honour the inaugural recipients in the fall of 2021. Please consider our situation and our proposal below.

The Faculty of Arts & Science Department of Slavic Languages and Literatures is the only Slavic department in Canada and ranks among the top departments in North America. Now more than 70 years old, it is renowned for the breadth of its undergraduate and graduate courses.

The department is building on its strengths while renewing curricula to reflect dramatic social, cultural, and political changes in Central, Eastern, and Southern Europe. Our department continues to develop new areas of research and expertise, and the insights we provide make it possible to better understand this strategically important region.

Our internationally recognized and award-winning faculty teach and research the languages and cultures of Central and Eastern Europe (Czech, Polish, Russian, South Slavic, and Ukrainian). We also work closely with a host of related Arts & Science departments such as history and political science, and research centres for drama, religion and linguistics, among others at the University of Toronto.

We are an invaluable access point and resource for the community. The U of T Library has over 500,000 volumes in its Slavic and East European collection, the most comprehensive collection of research materials in any Canadian library.

Our undergraduate students have achieved success in a wide range of fields and industries and our graduate students — who come from across Canada and around the world — have been hired by universities across the continent. We consistently rank among the top Arts & Science departments in surveys of student satisfaction.

Undergraduates can major or minor in almost any language area we teach. We have interdisciplinary courses that can also form part of the requirements for a major or minor in a specific stream.

In many ways, it is thanks to you the department has achieved its present shape, in part to serve the needs of the communities that have supported it over the years. In that regard, we would like to share an exciting opportunity for you to continue the partnership and support that has been crucial to its growth.

With a generous contribution of $25,000 (over five years) to your favourite department program, you can create an endowed award with a name chosen by you. The annual named award of $1,000 will be used to encourage undergraduate students already taking Slavic department courses to enroll in a major or specialist degree.

For a thoughtful gift of $12,500 (over five years), you can create a five-year expendable award recognized by a name of your choosing to encourage an undergraduate to enroll in a major or specialist degree in Slavic languages and cultures.

In either case, your special contribution to the program you support will be recognized by an award either in your name or as a tribute to a friend, loved one, or special occasion, for example, The [name chosen by donor] fellowship for the advanced study of _________ languages and cultures in your preferred program:

  • Czech and Slovak
  • Polish
  • Russian
  • South Slavic
  • Ukrainian
Eligibility for these fellowships will not be restricted by Ontario Student Opportunity Trust Fund (OSOTF) status or by financial need or by provincial residency or citizenship status. This is a competitive fellowship and the number of fellowships available through private donations will determine the number of recipients in each language area. Please consider this opportunity to support and encourage a new generation of students to enroll in the specific department program of your choice.

Thank you so very much! The named awards initiative will continue the goal of recruiting more undergraduates. We know that financial incentives are often the deciding factor when undergraduate students are deciding on what major or specialist course of studies to pursue. The fellowships will make an immediate and important impact.

As the 2020 tax season comes to a close, we are deeply grateful for your generosity. If your circumstances allow, please make the gift of a fellowship to our Slavic Languages Undergraduate Fellowship Fund.

With sincere thanks,

Professor Donna Orwin, Chair
Department of Slavic Languages and Literatures
Fellow Royal Society of Canada
