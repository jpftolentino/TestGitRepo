A message from President David Sylvester

And for all this, nature is never spent;
There lives the dearest freshness deep down things;
And though the last lights off the black West went
Oh, morning, at the brown brink eastward, springs—
(Hopkins, Grandeur of God)

Dear Alumni and Friends,

One of the beautiful things about being a part of the University of St. Michael’s College is our attentiveness to the rhythms of life in community. Our pastoral urban campus with its historic oak trees, colourful ivy, and beautiful rock gardens keeps us attentive to nature and demands that we acknowledge the passage of seasons.

The academic year is traditionally framed, of course, by all the necessary signposts of the academic year, such as move-in days, reading weeks, and midterm and final exams. But St. Michael’s is also defined by the rich cultural and religious mosaic of our community, and we are invited throughout the year to celebrate the many important feast days that help our students, faculty, and staff bring meaning to their work and studies: solemn and joyful holidays like Easter, Ramadan, Diwali, Rosh Hashanah, and Nowruz. Even as I write, friends and colleagues are preparing to light candles to mark Hanukkah.

So, here we are together now in Advent. For Christians, we wait and prepare to celebrate the birth of Christ, the light in the darkness, our hope for the world. For all of us, this is meant to be a time for completion, for rest and reflection with friends and family, and for preparations for new beginnings.

As I take stock of this past year, my thoughts are filled with gratitude. In this most remarkable year, in which all of us have experienced disruption, loss, and sadness, I am continually amazed by the dedication, kindness, and generosity of the people with whom I work. Their compassionate leadership and sacrifice have bolstered fellow students, professors, and colleagues alike. I see it every day in the bold actions and quiet kindnesses they take on. And I know that much more is done unseen.

The response to this pandemic from our students, faculty, staff, trustees, and alumni invites more than gratitude, however. It gives me great hope for the future of our community, and for our role in continuing to bring hope to others. Many challenges remain ahead of us, but the compassionate resilience of St. Michael’s shines through and sustains us even in these darkest days of winter. I now approach each day understanding that our university serves as a light of hope in these difficult days, for each other, and for the society in which we live.

As we pause to celebrate the coming holidays, I wish each of you and your families a happy and holy Christmas, and prayerful best wishes for a New Year marked by many new reasons to give thanks and to share in the hope that binds us all together.

David Sylvester, PhD
President and Vice-Chancellor
usmc.presidentsoffice@utoronto.ca

Welcoming Maxine to the Alumni Programs Team


Maxine Webster joined the St. Michael’s staff on November 30th, serving as Advancement Officer, Alumni Engagement, while Brittany Davila goes on maternity leave until January 2022. She will be managing and building on various alumni initiatives including virtual events and programming, social media and communications, and volunteer activity through the Alumni Association Board and Young Alumni Committee.

Prior to St. Mike’s, Maxine worked in higher education for 5 years. She served as Admissions Counselor and Preview Day Program Coordinator at The King’s College in New York City, where she graduated with a B.A. in Media, Culture, and the Arts in 2016. As WE Schools Program Manager at WE Charity, she managed relationships with 150+ partners, created and executed a digital communication plan, and supported the implementation of an experiential service-learning program.

Maxine looks forward to connecting with alumni in the months ahead and invites you to join with her and the Advancement team in wishing Brittany health and happiness in this exciting new chapter.

New Theology Courses at St. Mike’s


Two important new Faculty of Theology courses are demonstrating yet again that Theology is one of the timeliest of disciplines, using its insights and understanding to respond to current crises.

Black Lives Matter in the Classroom, a Basic Degree-level course to be taught by Dr. Marie Green, will address how educators can become better aware of—and better able to respond to—systemic injustices facing Black students at school.

At the Advanced Degree level, meanwhile, Faculty member Dr. Hilda Koster will be teaching Eco-Feminism, New Materialism and Ecological Theology at a time when the pandemic continues to remind us that we are not immune, not disconnected from the world around us. Read the entire story online.

Join the USMC Alumni Association Board


Do you have ideas about how to engage, celebrate, and strengthen alumni connections at St. Mike’s? Would you like to learn more about St. Mike’s plans and priorities and offer your perspectives?

A strong Alumni Association Board makes for a stronger St. Mike’s, and we are seeking new board members to help bring your voices to the table, champion your passions, and develop alumni programming geared to alumni like you.

The mission of the University of St. Michael’s College Alumni Association Board is to create and nurture relationships between the University and its alumni, as well as within the alumni community. The Board also represents alumni perspectives on decisions that affect the future of St. Mike’s.

For more information about Board membership, please contact Maxine Webster, Advancement Officer, at maxine.webster@utoronto.ca.

Mentor St. Mike’s Students


In this challenging year, St. Mike’s students need more support than ever, and we know St. Mike’s alumni can provide valuable insights into how you engaged with your network, established your careers, changed career paths, or allowed your personal interests to lead you to new opportunities.

As an alumni mentor, you can provide students with valuable perspectives and encouragement by sharing your experiences. This year, instead of arranging face-to-face meetings between students and alumni mentors, we are scheduling small (five to ten students) group Zoom sessions. These one-hour meetings will take place in February. If you are interested in participating as a mentor or would like more information, please contact Maxine Webster, Advancement Officer, at maxine.webster@utoronto.ca.
