Dear ##OptionalParameter12##,

As I approach the end of my first year as Dean of the Faculty of Arts & Science, I am deeply grateful to all members of our remarkable community. Arts & Science students, staff, faculty and, most importantly, all of you are showing tremendous fortitude during these unprecedented times, demonstrating how communities can unite in the face of adversity.

Today, I want to thank you for the part you have played over the past year in building a strong A&S network through your generous student mentorship.

Our students consistently express how invaluable it is to receive the support and guidance of alumni. By sharing your experience and expertise, you have educated and encouraged our students, helping them explore their career interests and introducing them to potential new paths. Along the way, your openness and reassurance are instilling in them the confidence they need to succeed in their own journeys.

We often hear from our alumni community that you volunteer because you benefitted from mentorship when you were students and now want to offer that same guidance and encouragement to others. Hearing this always fills me with pride and hope. This cycle of alumni support builds and enriches a strong global community and sense of connectedness, which is more important now than ever before as we work towards greater inclusivity, understanding of lived experiences, and diversity.

Our Backpack to Briefcase Industry Nights are a wonderful example of bringing people together. Last year, more than 340 students attended these events — highlighting how important students view opportunities to meet successful alumni and see firsthand how the time and effort they put into their education can influence their future success. These events resonate with you as well, since more than 150 alumni attended this series. We deeply appreciate the time you give to our students who are eager to be inspired by your wisdom and experiences.

We are so grateful for the many ways in which alumni volunteers give back to A&S. Whether you share your experiences with current or prospective students at events, connect with students online or host students at your place of business (when we are able to do so again), you continue to make meaningful contributions to the lives of our students. Thank you for so generously offering your time and wisdom.

During the pandemic and with everything taking place virtually, we are coming up with new and creative ways to stay connected, like U of T’s hub on Ten Thousand Coffees. If you’re interested in learning more about how you can continue to support students and new grads, I encourage you to fill out the alumni volunteer survey.

On behalf of the entire A&S community, thank you once again for your participation in our alumni volunteer programs. I hope you and your loved ones are staying safe and keeping healthy as we continue to face — and overcome — today’s challenges together.


Yours,

Melanie A. Woodin
Dean, Faculty of Arts & Science
Professor, Department of Cell & Systems Biology
