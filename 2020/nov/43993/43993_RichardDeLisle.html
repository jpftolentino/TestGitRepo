Dear Colleagues,

I hope you and your loved ones are well. In this time of COVID-19, we are reminded every day how much we need each other.

The pandemic has taught us how resilient we are while underscoring the power of community. Even as circumstances compel us to work and live much differently, we are surrounded by opportunity, a chance to reaffirm the core values we hold dear.

With A&S advancing solutions to the most pressing issues of these times, I am so proud of the positive impact our students, faculty, staff and alumni continue to have on the world around us. And as a U of T alumna, faculty member and dean, I am reminded every day how lucky I am to be a part of this Faculty, the heart of U of T, helping students reach their potential. They give me hope.

But I also know many students are facing unexpected costs and financial emergencies. They need help with housing, job loss, food insecurity and added technology needs. They may be at risk of dropping out of their studies. Student scholarships and bursaries alleviate stress and encourage great achievements.

Now more than ever, we need your help to ensure the brightest and best students can access a U of T education, regardless of their financial situation. Your support of students and student initiatives is critically important. As is advancing our world-class research programs. Donations to unique research projects provide vital resources for groundbreaking work in the humanities, sciences and social sciences for which the Faculty is renowned.

Each fall, we reach out to you, our generous faculty and staff, to ask for your support. This year, we understand these are challenging times for everyone, but I hope you will consider giving. No gift is too small and will support the amazing things happening here every day.

Thank you, as always, for all that you do to support Arts & Science.



Yours,

Professor Melanie Woodin
Dean, Faculty of Arts & Science
University of Toronto
