TOP STORIES

Ethiopic program reaches $500,000 endowment goal, thanks to community support — and another gift from The Weeknd

The future of the Ethiopic program — the only one of its kind in North America and among very few in the world — just got brighter. Recent support, including from The Weeknd, means U of T can offer at least one Ge’ez language course each year.

“Our heartfelt thanks to The Weeknd for his ongoing commitment to Ethiopic studies at U of T,” said Professor Melanie Woodin, dean of the Faculty of Arts & Science.

U of T community celebrates remarkable Class of 2020

“Clearly no challenge is too great for the Class of 2020.” These words, delivered by fall convocation keynote speaker Professor Maydianne Andrade, wonderfully capture the spirit and drive of U of T’s Class of 2020. On November 21, the newest members of U of T’s alumni family were celebrated by fellow graduands and the greater U of T community in a virtual ceremony, starting with a virtual event hosted by Arts & Science Dean Melanie Woodin. Read about what they accomplished, how they celebrated — and what they’re doing next.

A&S alumnus proud to play part in promising COVID-19 vaccine

An A&S alumnus has played a key role in the development of Moderna’s COVID-19 vaccine, which has demonstrated 94.5 per cent effectiveness.

Derrick Rossi, who earned his bachelor of science and master of science from U of T and co-founded Moderna, helped pioneer the use of modified messenger RNA (or mRNA) to produce selected proteins — technology the Toronto Star reports is central to Moderna’s experimental vaccine.

Alumni authors celebrated in 2020 Giller Prize

A&S alumni clearly have the “write stuff.” Souvankham Thammavongsa is the winner of the 2020 Scotiabank Giller Prize for her short story collection, How To Pronounce Knife, and Gil Adamson is a finalist for her novel Ridgerunner.

Read more about what it means to receive such a prestigious honour and what’s next for these celebrated authors.

HIGHLIGHTS

New U of T partnership to boost economy, help workers develop skills

U of T and Palette Inc., a national non-profit organization, have launched a partnership to develop bold new strategies to equip people with the tools they need to succeed in Canada’s rapidly changing economy — including those impacted by COVID-19.

Palette recently welcomed Dean Melanie Woodin as its newest board member.

Alumnus heads to familiar grounds for inspiration

U of T’s Robarts Library provided the inspiration alumnus David Pelech needed as he finished the script for Decoys, featured on CBC’s Gem streaming service.

“When you see all those students around you really focusing, you can’t help but wonder if you’re working hard enough,” says Pelech, who wrote Decoys and stars as one of the main characters.

THE IMPACT OF GIVING

Under-represented computer science students get boost from U.K.-based AI firm

Four new graduate scholarships for under-represented students have been established in the Department of Computer Science, thanks to a recent gift from U.K.-based artificial intelligence company, DeepMind.

“We want AI to be an inclusive technology, and that means it must be shaped by a range of different perspectives,” says Obum Ekeke, University and Educational Partnerships lead at DeepMind.

Staff and faculty open up about why they support U of T

Just ahead of Giving Tuesday on December 1, U of T faculty and staff shared their powerful reasons for supporting U of T.

The hardships highlighted by COVID-19, helping attract the best specialists to the University and the immediate impact support has on students are just a few of their reasons — and the list goes on.

“I am very happy that I’m able to contribute to some of these funds to support them,” says Department of Philosophy Chair Martin Pickavé.
