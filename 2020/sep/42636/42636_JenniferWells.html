In this edition:

The University of Toronto welcomes students, faculty and staff back to campus — safely and at times virtually — for a fall term like no other. We’ve developed an excellent e-learning strategy that brings together expertise from across Arts & Science and the university at large.

In this issue, we’re excited to be “Leveraging Our Strengths” over the next five years with our academic plan, and we explore the rich Indigenous history of the University’s three campuses. We celebrate the creation of the Professor Linda Munk Graduate Futures Scholarship, one of the largest scholarships in Canada for English master of arts students, and recognize the impact Donald “Digger” Gorman had throughout his career. Plus: online events, e-mentoring and so much more!

Stay well — and we look forward to seeing you again in person when it’s safe to do so.

TOP STORIES

New term, new approach — same commitment to excellence

We’re welcoming students safely to campus for the fall term, whether they’re returning or joining us for the first time. Also, read about two A&S professors who are so committed to developing the best online learning experience for their students that they offered free seminars this summer for practice.

And the pandemic won’t prevent students from exploring the world this fall through virtual field trips, digital labs and partnerships with students in Mexico.

We’re welcoming first-year students

First-year students had access to a wide range of new courses, programs and workshops to help them feel welcome and engaged at a challenging time.

“Planning for the start of this academic year has been like no other in our history,” says Melanie Woodin, dean of the Faculty of Arts & Science. “We have reimagined every aspect of the U of T experience, from how we teach and learn, to how we connect, socialize and stay informed.

Beloved Professor Linda Munk’s love of literature lives on in new scholarship

A generous gift from the family of the late Professor Emeritus Linda Munk has created the Professor Linda Munk Graduate Futures Scholarship, one of the largest scholarships in Canada for English master of arts students.

“U of T’s Department of English is the perfect place to recognize our mother,” says Marc-David Munk.

We’re Leveraging Our Strengths — and we need you!

Arts & Science is set to leverage our considerable strengths as U of T’s largest academic division through our 2020-2025 academic plan.

“Your voices have informed these priorities for A&S and will be instrumental in implementing ‘Leveraging our Strengths,’” says Melanie Woodin, dean of the Faculty of Arts & Science.

Learn about our six overarching priorities for the next five years in “Leveraging our Strengths.”

HIGHLIGHTS

Virtual reality brings Earth sciences field trip to students’ homes

Second-year Earth sciences students didn’t miss out on the two-week field course planned to take place near White Fish Falls, near Sudbury.

Students learned key geological skills during August, thanks to a VR version of the course.

A&S mentors connecting virtually with students and new alumni

Our Backpack to Briefcase (b2B) Summer Alumni Speaker Series continues to connect A&S mentors with new alumni and students, despite the pandemic.

Thanks to affinity sponsors TD Insurance and MNBA, the fall series will include a combination of online sessions recorded by alumni and students.

New “gateway to campus” planned for 90 Queen’s Park transformation

U of T and members of the public are working with the City of Toronto to usher in a new era with an extraordinary building planned for 90 Queen’s Park.

The building will be home to a range of A&S departments and units.

Learn more about this new gateway to campus.

Professor Jon Johnson leads an Indigenous history tour last year, pre-COVID.

Students celebrate U of T’s rich Indigenous history

A group of Indigenous U of T students and young adults from across the GTA are set to lead virtual tours to celebrate the rich Indigenous history of the University's three campuses and surrounding areas. “My interest in this is really in my identity and reconnecting with that,” says Drew Rickard, a Cree First Nation member, third-year A&S student and one of the tour guides.

The tours are part of an ongoing partnership between U of T and First Story Toronto, formerly known as the Toronto Native Community History Project.

Writers share a new view of what it means to be Muslim in new A&S
e-magazine

Where is Islam found? In mosques, yes, but also in coffee shops and sports bars. Islam in the City, a new A&S e-magazine, is challenging common perceptions of what it means to be Muslim in Toronto, thanks to the Institute of Islamic Studies. The first issue features “A New Love” by Alina Butt, pictured.

A&S researchers explore complex problems as Canada Research Chairs

Eight A&S researchers have been named Canada Research Chairs for their scholarly excellence, and are tackling complex topics such as early Alzheimer’s disease detection, pollution control policy and the evolution and death of massive stars.

A&S chairholders represent more than a quarter of the 29 U of T researchers named in the latest cohort, with U of T holding the greatest number of research chairs in the country.

 
 
THE IMPACT OF GIVING

“Digger” Gorman’s impact continues through scholarship created in his name

Tributes are pouring in for A&S Professor Donald “Digger” Gorman, who passed away in April.

As we remember Digger’s illustrious geological career, we also celebrate the impact he’ll continue to have in the lives of students through the D.H. Gorman Explorers Fund Graduate Scholarship created in his honour.

Alumna’s gift to ‘Friends of Patrick’ fund boosts brain research for grad students

Graduate students are advancing cognitive neuroscience research, thanks to a recent gift to the Friends of Patrick fMRI Brain Acceleration Fund.

“Friends of Patrick will be a constant reminder to reflect upon a young man who, while unable to attend university, is a teacher to many,” says Lucinda Widdrington, who helped create the fund in her son’s name.
