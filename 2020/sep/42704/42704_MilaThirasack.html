Welcome to the new academic year, and what is likely the strangest fall term ever. While campus is gradually opening up, almost all our courses will be online only this fall, and our faculty and staff will continue to work mainly from home for the time being. While we’ll miss all the usual opportunities to meet each other face to face, the school is working hard to produce a series of online seminars, symposia and other events that will still bring us together. We’d love to hear your ideas for other ways we can all stay connected.
 
Despite the difficulties brought on by the pandemic, it will be an exciting year for the School of the Environment. We welcomed three new faculty members over the summer, and we’re working on a series of renovations of our space while the offices are closed to create more of a hub for teaching and research at the school. We’re also planning a significant change in the school’s status this year that will allow us to hire more of our own faculty and to play a stronger role in interdisciplinary work on environment and sustainability across the university — look out for news of this soon. And of course, we’re working toward accepting the first students into our new master's program next summer.
 
So, welcome to everyone joining the School of the Environment this year, and welcome back to everyone else. Stay safe!
 
— Professor Steve Easterbrook, Director, School of the Environment

NEW FACULTY
The School is excited to welcome three new faculty members. 
Prof. Vianey Leos Barajas, joint with the Department of Statistical Sciences, is a statistician whose primary area of research is statistical ecology, with a focus on modelling of animal movement. She analyzes shark and sheep movements, along with snake tracking data. Most recently, she has begun work in the area of environmental statistics with a focus on spatial modelling of disease data. 
Prof. Robert Soden, joint with the Department of Computer Science, works on crisis informatics, human-centred computing, and science and technology studies. His research uses critical and participatory design tactics to evaluate and improve the technologies we use to understand and respond to environmental challenges like disasters and climate change.
 
Prof. Nicole Spiegelaar, joint with Trinity College, is the Assistant Director of the Trinity Sustainability Initiative, which connects the classroom, student research, internships and campus sustainability through experiential learning. She works with complex adaptive systems theory and interdisciplinary knowledge integration to address sustainability challenges.

Professors Steve Easterbrook and Miriam Diamond speak to the effects of climate change on air quality in the Toronto Star article Those smoky skies over Vancouver are a glimpse of the future. "We know there is a very clear link between climate change and wildfires, because hotter, drier conditions allow fires to spread more widely,” said Prof. Easterbrook.

Findings from the paper The Widespread Environmental Footprint of Indigo Denim Microfibers from Blue Jeans co-authored by Prof. Miriam Diamond was featured extensively in the media including CBC Online, CTV, WIRED and the New Scientist. It examines the footprint of modern blue jean society by investigating the environmental distribution, pathways and sources of indigo denim microfibers shed by denim clothing.
Prof. Stephen Scharper will serve as the Director of the Trinity Sustainability Initiative (TSI) for an initial three-year term. The TSI, a collaboration between Trinity College and the School of the Environment, aims to integrate sustainability across the college and the campus, and was made possible by a $10-million landmark gift from alumni Brian and Joannah Lawson.

COMMUNITY EVENTS
Hosted by the Committee on the Environment, Climate Change, and Sustainability (CECCS), the Adams Sustainability Celebration is a tribute to the growing community of sustainability-minded students, staff and faculty at U of T.  The purpose of this event series is to celebrate successes, inspire new activities, and collaboratively deepen campus engagement around sustainability. The celebration will feature sustainability-related panel sessions on Tuesdays, asynchronous showcases, the Adams Sustainability Innovation Prize competition, grant opportunities and more!
