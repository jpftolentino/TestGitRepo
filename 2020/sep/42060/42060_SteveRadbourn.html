What do Isabel Bayrakdarian, Jeffrey Skoll, and Jonny Sun have in common? Besides all being wildly successful in their respective domains (and hailing from your own alma matter), they all, at one time or another, have had mentors and been mentors.

Mentorship is an ingredient for success that lends a competitive advantage - successful people need mentorship and are mentors. Knowledge sharing, passed down from generation to generation, creates strong communities filled with empowered and capable people, like you.

The U of T Engineering Alumni Mentorship Program (AMP) is a robust way for you to be make a significant impact on your global community, and the future of a bright U of T Engineering student, especially needed during this coming academic year of virtual learning.

Why Become a Mentor with AMP? Three reasons:
  1. Being a mentor is a rewarding experience
  2. Opportunity to create lasting impact on the next generation
  3. Just a little of your time goes a long way– as little as grabbing a cup of coffee or having a phone call now and then (or more if you chose).
2020-2021 students require more than just career guidance. They seek to talk with professionals who were once in their place. Imagine being able to tell yourself as an undergraduate the things you know now? Valuable. Plus, you can expand your network, meet interesting people, and learn. As we navigate the COVID-19 pandemic, mentor virtually from anywhere in the world!

How to Become a Mentor with AMP:
  • Register for the Alumni Mentorship Program online information session on September 16 to learn more about the program.
  • Join U of T Engineering CONNECT. Save your credentials in your trusted device. Wait for a membership approval email. Log in and select ‘’Groups’’ on the left hand menu, ‘’Alumni Mentorship Program 2020-21’’ and ‘’join group’’. This expresses your commitment to the program for the academic year.
    • If you’re already on CONNECT, you have taken the first step! All you need to do is go to the “Groups” section and join the “Alumni Mentorship Program 2020-21” to participate for the year.
    • Keep your profile up to date and include a professional, approachable profile image.
  • We will be holding a virtual Mentorship Kick-Off Event on October 7 where you will have an opportunity to meet the student you’ll be mentoring.
  • If you’re not in a time zone that suits attending the kick off event, register anyway and view the recording a few days later, which will be sent to you as a result. Note that the AMP is now on our Engineering CONNECT online platform, which means you can be a mentor no matter where you live. It also enables our students to match with mentors who best reflect their goals.
We sincerely hope you’ll do us the honour of joining the Alumni Mentorship Program. We also welcome you to join the AMP Reddit forum, where alumni and students are invited to chat on mentorship ideas and initiatives. The deadline to be matched with a student in time for our Kick-Off Event is September 30, 2020. If you miss that date, don’t worry, we welcome alumni mentors to join throughout the year, with dozens of singular mentorship events, volunteer judge and assessor opportunities and much more. However, to get the most out of the AMP, we suggest you apply on or before the Kick-Off Event.

Would like to provide some feedback? Contact me directly at luigi.lacorte@mail.utoronto.ca
