The Physics Third- & Fourth-﻿Year Mentorship Program

Have you ever thought about being a mentor?

Do you want to give back to the physics community by sharing your experiences and helping a bright physics student? The program is:

  • Open to students in the U of T St. George Campus physics majors, specialist, and joint specialist programs;
  • Aimed at students considering their career and research paths;
  • Designed to connect our students with U of T physics alumni and members of the physics community who are working in industry or academia and who want to share their insight and experience;
  • Run virtually due to COVID-19, until face-to-face meetings and gatherings are possible again; and
  • Dependant on at least four virtual meetings between you and your mentee from October to March to fulfill the requirements of the University of Toronto Co-Curricular Record Program. However, you can meet more frequently if you and your mentee would like.

Past mentors have said:

“Helping students figure out their priorities and giving them good advice is super rewarding. I aim to keep getting better at advising students.”

“I like vicariously enjoying the plans and accomplishments of my mentees.”

“I enjoy helping physics students focus on what they want to do once they obtain a degree and prepare themselves for the real world of competing for jobs and how to network to get the job they really want.”

From a mentee in the 2019/2020 program:

“My mentor was able to help me out with a lot of my concerns and questions; he was in a position where I aim to be in the future, and helped me get on the right path toward achieving that goal. Furthermore, he helped guide me to make the right decisions on matters I was unsure about.”
