Winter 2020

New look!

We hope everyone got off to a great start to 2020! And since we’re starting a new decade, we thought it was time to refresh and redesign our newsletter for A&S alumni and friends. We hope you find it an easier and more compelling read about what’s happening at Arts & Science, and the incredible impact of our remarkable students, faculty and alumni, here and around the world. And, of course, we’ll update you on the many ways our donors support the great initiatives here as well. Send us your feedback and story ideas to: artsci.communications@utoronto.ca

Earth sciences students visit Turkey where geology and culture mix, and continents collide

To an aspiring geologist, southern Ontario leaves something to be desired sitting astride the geologically stable Canadian Shield. Not so the Cappadocia region of central Turkey. Professors Russell Pysklywec and Grant Henderson, of the Department of Earth Sciences, led a group of 13 A&S students to this tectonic trove for the department’s Minerals and Rocks International/Indigenous Course Module.

One gift leads to another — new MGA scholarship to help Caribbean students

On a recent flight to Toronto, Connie Carter beamed as she filled out her customs form. In the box that asked for her nationality, she was able to print “Canadian” for the very first time. “I felt such pride,” says Carter, who was born in Jamaica, educated in the United Kingdom, lived in Denmark for most of her adult life, and became a Canadian citizen last October. She celebrated by creating a scholarship.

Friday, February 14, 2020
10:00 am – 12:30 pm
Principal Hall
1 Spadina Crescent, Toronto
