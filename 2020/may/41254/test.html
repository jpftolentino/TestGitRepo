Spring 2020

In this edition:

The University of Toronto makes history with its first virtual Convocation, good news stories in a COVID-19 world, staying connected and giving back, and an A&S-themed crossword contest — just for you! But first, U of T expresses solidarity with the Black community and condemns the anti-Black racism and violence occurring around the world.

We hope you are well and supported during these challenging times.

Top Stories

U of T expresses solidarity with the Black community

As protests around the world take place against anti-Black racism and violence, U of T stands united with the Black community in denouncing anti-Black racism and discrimination in all its forms.

Heartfelt congratulations, creative celebrations for the resilient Class of 2020

On June 2, U of T celebrated the Class of 2020 with the first convocation in the university’s 193-year history in which all degrees were conferred in absentia — and it all started with a rousing Arts & Science Watch Party.

‘The entire world coming together’: U of T alumni’s website tracks COVID-19 research – and recoveries

Trinity alumna Juliana Lee realized she had been posting so much negative news about the pandemic on Instagram and Facebook since March that she was becoming anxious and depressed. So she launched a website focused on positive recovery news and more.

A&S faculty receive support from Toronto COVID-19 Action Fund

The University of Toronto has launched the $8.4-million Toronto COVID-19 Action Fund to support high-impact projects by researchers from U of T and its hospital partners. After a fast-tracked 30-day peer review process, 31 projects — including two in Arts & Science — were selected:

• Chemistry chair Richard Batey’s work to support ongoing COVID-19 research and target new opportunities for therapeutic intervention; and

• Sociology chair Scott Schieman’s work to better understand COVID-19 consequences on Canadians’ work, economic life and health over the next five years.

Mentors matter: International students cope with COVID-19

While COVID-19 has disrupted the lives of students across U of T’s Faculty of Arts & Science, international students whose homes are time zones away have been especially impacted. Sudden travel restrictions gave them little time to decide whether to stay or return home. Katie Kwang describes her whirlwind days after the lockdown — and the impact of support from a mentor.

Highlights

#DisplayYourPride – What’s your alumni story?

We’re celebrating Pride at U of T and June 18 is #DisplayYourPride day, when students, faculty and staff share in the celebrations, online and on our social media channels. We’re inviting our alumni to join us. Are you a change-maker, leader, or volunteer in the LGTBQ+ community? Tell us about what you, or a fellow U of T alum, is doing to advocate for greater inclusion and diversity in Canada, and around the world. Send your story ideas to: artsci.communications@utoronto.ca and we may follow up to feature it!

What is the Faculty planning for the fall term?

Melanie Woodin, dean of the Faculty of Arts & Science, shares her thoughts on how the Faculty has adjusted to the COVID-19 pandemic and what undergraduate students can expect as we look ahead to the Fall Session and the start of a new academic year.

A&S launches Online Learning Academy

The Faculty of Arts & Science includes several faculty members acclaimed for their use of technology in teaching. In response to our rapidly changing environment, the Faculty has launched the A&S Online Learning Academy to bring these professors and instructors together to share their expertise in providing outstanding online learning opportunities for our students.

‘Science Sam’ and her new 3Qs at the U video series

You can connect with and learn from University of Toronto experts in a new weekly video series hosted by U of T and Arts & Science alumna Samantha Yammine, the neuroscientist and science communicator better known as Science Sam. Yammine will host 3Qs at the U with some of the university’s top researchers, asking three questions of each to better understand their area of expertise.

Looking back: Urban planning and the impact of disease

What can urban planning history tell us about the impact of disease? How have past infectious diseases affected urban density? Richard White, an independent historian and longstanding part-time lecturer in Canadian history at the University of Toronto Mississauga and University of Toronto Scarborough, shares his thoughts on the impact of disease on urban planning history.

Looking forward: Will Toronto change permanently as a result of the pandemic?

How will Toronto evolve from after COVID-19? Will it change how people work and live in this city? How will local economies rebound? Matti Siemiatycki, an associate professor in the Faculty of Arts & Science’s Department of Geography & Planning and the interim director of the School of Cities, discusses how the pandemic may change the Toronto of tomorrow.

Studying together at a distance, thanks to alumnus Nasir Kharma’s popular YouTube channel

Arts & Science alumnus Nasir Kharma isn’t letting the COVID-19 pandemic keep him from studying with students around the world — virtually, of course. To help students stay motivated and connected to their academics at a time when many are feeling more isolated than ever, he posted a seven-hour YouTube video of himself studying that was viewed a remarkable 130,000 times in the first week alone.

What’s behind puzzles’ popularity during the pandemic?

For some, the rise of jigsaw puzzle sales during the COVID-19 pandemic may be, well, puzzling. Marcel Danesi, a professor of semiotics (the study of signs and symbols) and author of several books on puzzles, pieces together this trend.

Why Give?

A&S celebrates 35 years of empowering women — thanks to donor support

For 35 years, the City of Toronto Women and Gender Studies Scholarship has been empowering Faculty of Arts & Science students to accomplish amazing things.

“This scholarship has been a really important way of recognizing the outstanding work our students are doing and its impact on the world outside the classroom and university,” says Alissa Trotz, director of the Faculty of Arts & Science’s Women & Gender Studies Institute.

Capturing Canada’s rich — and eccentric — biographies

For 61 years, the Dictionary of Canadian Biography has been telling Canadians’ stories. So far, its inventory includes nearly 9,000 prominent, influential and just plain colourful characters from Canadian history.

“We pride ourselves on being authoritative,” says Chris Pennington, one of two manuscript editors at the dictionary’s Toronto office.

Thinking of up-skilling? U of T launches $100,000 Opportunity Fund for students

U of T’s School of Continuing Studies wants to help those experiencing financial challenges due to COVID-19 to pursue their education and upgrade their skills. The Fund provides up to $750 to each eligible student. Almost half of the School of Continuing Studies courses are available online and by remote delivery.
