“ Preparing for COVID-29 ”
The COVID-19 crisis seemed to take the world by surprise. This talk will examine, among other topics, how ill-prepared we were for a pandemic’s impact on the economy. While ending the pandemic is an ongoing discussion, Professor Gans takes a longer-term perspective, asking what we have learned that can guide our investment practices before the next one.
Joshua Gans, PhD in Economics, Stanford University, holds the Jeffrey S. Skoll Chair in Technical Innovation and Entrepreneurship and is a professor at the Rotman School of Management and the Department of Economics. His research is focused on understanding the economic drivers of innovation and scientific progress, with interests in digital strategy and antitrust policy. Prof. Gans is the department editor (strategy) of Management Science, managing director of Core Research consultancy, and writes regularly for HBR and Digitopoly. He recently published The Pandemic Information Gap: The Brutal Economics of Covid-19 (MIT Press).
