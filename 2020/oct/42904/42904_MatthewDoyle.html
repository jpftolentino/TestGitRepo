Dear Alumni and Friends,

I hope you and your loved ones have been staying healthy and safe throughout this most challenging time. My colleagues and I miss seeing you and welcoming you to alumni events. We look forward to hosting alumni gatherings again before too long. In the meantime, we’re exploring new ways to stay in touch, offer virtual events and programs, connect you with students and fellow alumni and as St. Mike’s 180 strategic planning continues involve you in envisioning St. Mike’s future.

This edition of our alumni e-newsletter focuses on ways you can connect with the St. Mike’s community as a volunteer, mentor, cheerleader, or even as a virtual student! To learn more about any of these opportunities or to get involved please contact us at: smc.alumniaffairs@utoronto.ca. We would love to hear from you.

Join the USMC Alumni Association Board


Do have ideas about how to engage alumni in life at St. Mike’s? Would you like to help organize new ways to celebrate and strengthen lasting connections from your time in residence or other aspects of campus life? Would you like to learn more about St. Mike’s plans and priorities and offer your perspectives? If so, please consider joining our Alumni Association Board.

The mission of the University of St. Michael’s College Alumni Association Board is to create and nurture relationships between the University and its alumni, as well as within the alumni community. The Board also represents alumni perspectives on decisions that affect the future of St. Mike’s.

We are seeking new board members to bring your voices to the table, champion your passions and help us develop alumni programming geared to alumni like you.

For more information about Board membership, please contact Brittany Davila, Senior Manager Alumni Programs at brittany.davila@utoronto.ca.

Welcome Ana Maria to the St. Mike’s Community


Meet Ana Maria Faria, who has recently joined St. Mike’s Advancement team as Director of Development. Ana Maria has extensive experience in fundraising and has a great sense of strategic planning, program development, and communications, developed through leadership roles at Frontier College, George Brown College and Easter Seals Canada, where she served as Vice President, National Development.

In addition to overseeing Major Giving and Gift Planning, Ana Maria will be working closely with Chief Advancement Officer Kathryn Elton to further develop funding priorities and to explore and advance new initiatives.

Ana Maria: “It is a very exciting time at St. Mike's, and I am so delighted to join a great team of talented individuals all working together to advance the mission and priorities of the University. I'm so looking forward to getting to know the alumni community.”

Become a Mentor to SMC students


After graduating from St. Mike’s, did you take a different path than you had originally planned? Have you established a career unrelated to your studies, changed careers, or seen your personal interests lead you to new opportunities?

If any of these scenarios ring true, you can provide students with valuable perspectives and encouragement by sharing your experiences and insights as an alumni mentor. This year, instead of arranging face-to-face meetings between students and alumni mentors, we are scheduling small (five to ten students) group Zoom sessions. These one-hour meetings will take place in February. If you are interested in participating as a mentor or would like more information, please contact Brittany Davila at brittany.davila@utoronto.ca.

Share Words of Encouragement


Reading Week is fast approaching! As alumni, you may remember long hours studying with fellow students at favourite spots on campus or in the library. This year, study space is limited due to COVID and many of our students are studying from home, away from classmates. Our Campus Life team is trying to lift students’ spirits by gathering messages of encouragement to display around campus and to share via social media. To share your words of encouragement please visit here to submit the form. You might also include a photo from your USMC days to show how much times have changed!

Send Video Messages to Student Athletes


Student athletes are also dealing with the disappointment of the cancelled fall season, and we invite alumni to offer encouraging words. We’ve launched a cheerleading campaign to share video messages with St. Mike’s student athletes. To submit a short video to reflect on your sports memories or to wish student athletes luck during their school year, visit https://stmikes.capsule.video/. We’ll be sending the videos to all our student athletes on November 20.

Audit Theology Classes for Free!


Have you ever wondered what it would be like to return to school? Now is your chance to dip your toe in and see what it’s like to head back to class at St. Mike’s.

All St. Mike’s alumni are eligible to audit up to three Faculty of Theology classes for free, with certain stipulations. To learn more about this wonderful opportunity, please contact Anthony De Feo, Faculty’s Programs Coordinator, at inquiry.usmctheology@utoronto.ca.

Send us Your News


Are you celebrating a new job, a promotion, a book, paper, or prize, engagement, marriage or new family member? If so, we want to hear from you.

If you have personal or professional news you’d like to share with the alumni community, send it along to smc.alumniaffairs@utoronto.ca. We’ll be delighted to share it, with your permission, in the Bulletin Board section of the St. Michael’s magazine, on social media or our LinkedIn page. And if you have a high-resolution photo to share, even better!

And please be sure to send any updates to your mailing or email address or phone number to smc.alumniaffairs@utoronto.ca. so we can stay in touch with you.
