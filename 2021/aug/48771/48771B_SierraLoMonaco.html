Dear ##OptionalParameter1##,

When speaking of mentorship, who better comes to mind than Mr. Miyagi from the Karate Kid (1984). From “Wax on, Wax off” to “It’s ok to lose to an opponent. It’s never okay to lose to fear”, Mr. Miyagi imparted wisdom using analogies to help us simplify and unravel the complexities of life. However, Mr. Miyagi is not the only individual students reference as being synonymous with mentorship, but rather the Alumni Mentorship Program (AMP) mentees will now reference you, as a past mentor, when mentorship is being discussed. As we approach the beginning of the 2021-2022 academic year, I would like to formally invite you to return to the U of T Engineering Alumni Mentorship Program as an Alumni Mentor.

We have spent the summer reviewing your invaluable feedback and consequently revamped the program according to your meaningful suggestions. To learn more about the changes to AMP and how to become an Alumni Mentor, please complete the following:
  1. Register for the Alumni Mentorship Program’s virtual Information Session on September 15
    • If you have a conflict with the Information Session event, register anyway, and view the recording a few days later, which we will share with you.
  2. Log into U of T Engineering CONNECT and select ‘’Groups’’ on the left-hand menu. Select ‘’Alumni Mentorship Program 2021-22’’ and ‘’join group’’ to expresses your commitment to the program for the 2021-2022 academic year. Furthermore, please ensure that your profile is up-to-date and include a professional profile image.
  3. Visit our Alumni Mentorship Program webpage for more information.
We sincerely hope you will do us the honour again of joining the Alumni Mentorship Program. Plus, should you decide to embrace Mr. Miyagi’s ways of mentorship while simultaneously delivering transformational work and life advice, you may be able to swindle a free car wash in the process. If you have any further questions, feedback, or additional ideas, please do not hesitate to contact me at luigi.lacorte@utoronto.ca.

Sincerely,

Luigi La Corte, Civ 1T4+PEY
Chair, Alumni Mentorship Program Committee
