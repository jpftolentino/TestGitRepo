President and Vice-Chancellor Dr. David Sylvester offers his congratulations to Kathryn Elton on her retirement:

A message from Kathryn Elton, Chief Advancement Officer:

Dear Alumni and Friends,

Joining the St. Mike’s community and collaborating with alumni, faculty, staff, and students to support and advance this great institution has been the personal and professional highlight of my career.

I love this community, and I am excited about all the great things that are happening here as the St. Mike’s 180: Rooted in the Future strategic plan unfolds. However, after turning sixty-four last April, I decided it is time to join my husband in retirement, move back home to Guelph, and pursue new adventures while I still have "gas in my tank."

The Advancement and Alumni Affairs team and I have spent time together this summer planning for the year ahead. A new team leader will be confirmed soon, and I will be assisting with the transition when they arrive.

For today, I want to share the news of my retirement with you and express my appreciation for the opportunity to work for, and with, St. Mike's, and for, and with, you! I hope our paths will cross as I stay connected with St. Mike’s as a true-blue fan and supporter.

Kathryn Elton, Chief Advancement Officer

Welcoming Modele to the Advancement and Alumni Affairs Team
 

Modele Kuforiji joined the St. Michael’s College staff on May 10, 2021, serving as Advancement Assistant. He assists with the implementation of alumni and donor events and programming, supports fundraising activities, and provides administrative support to the Advancement team.

Modele graduated from St. Mike’s in 2018 with a Bachelor of Arts in English and a Double Minor in Anthropology and Environmental Anthropology. After his undergraduate studies, he traveled to Amsterdam to pursue his Master of Social Cultural Anthropology. Prior to joining the team at St. Mike’s, he worked at U of T in the Engineering Outreach office as the Residence Life Coordinator.

Modele looks forward to connecting with alumni in the months ahead!

Celebrating Women Religious at St. Mike’s

It was women’s religious communities that opened the college doors for female students more than 100 years ago, with the arrival of the Loretto Sisters and the Sisters of St. Joseph as professors and advocates for women’s higher education. This site celebrates, through archival materials and historical accounts, how these two religious communities changed the face of St. Michael’s.

To celebrate the anniversaries of the Loretto Sisters and the Sisters of St. Joseph, alumni and friends are invited to tune in to a series of virtual conversations beginning in October, celebrating women religious at St. Mike’s. More details to follow in September.

We remain hopeful that in-person alumni programming will return in the spring. Until then, we are planning virtual gatherings and presentations. Please visit the website for updates and information on campus reopening plans.
