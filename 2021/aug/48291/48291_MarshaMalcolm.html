A message from Selina Esteves
I hope this issue finds you healthy and enjoying all the wonders of summer. As our means of connection continue to be virtual, our commitment to communicate with our alumni remains. In this issue, we share details about a research study on the effects of tooth whitening, look at the experiences of several alumni through the pandemic including a new grad who has practiced for her first full year and an alumnus who serves a northern Indigenous community. Additionally, alumni share their thoughts on the qualitative research study being undertaken by advancement and we present a story about how the Faculty mentored Black and Indigenous high school students this summer who are interested in the profession of dentistry. With back- to- school activities around the corner, we also wanted to share an update on our plans for the fall.

As we look ahead to the fall term, the Faculty of Dentistry will be taking a hybrid approach to lectures with a mix of in-person and virtual delivery. We expect our labs to be back to full capacity and are excited about the opening of our new Satellite Clinic at 777 Bay Street. While we are prepared for all of these in-person activities, we understand that Public Health and University guidelines may change or evolve to continue to ensure the safety of our students, staff, faculty and patients. For the most up-to-date information during these uncertain times, please visit our website.
