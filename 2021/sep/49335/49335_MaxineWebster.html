Dear Alumni and Friends,

I hope you and your loved ones enjoyed a healthy, safe, and wonderful summer.

While activities slowed down on campus in July and August—and the pandemic made things more quiet than usual—there was still a lot to celebrate at St. Michael’s, including the appointment of Dr. Cynthia Cameron as the new Keenan Chair of Religious Education and Assistant Professor of Religious Education, Father Andrew Summerson joining the Faculty of Theology as Assistant Professor of Greek Patristics, and welcoming Sonal Castelino as the new Director of Campus Ministry, among other exciting news.

This month, we welcomed the class of 2025 to the St. Michael’s community. Campus tours and several orientation events—including paint nights and movie screenings—could be done in-person, and nearly 800 incoming first year and returning second year students took part. Campus Life also organized virtual orientation events, and nearly 1,000 incoming first year students got involved.

We remain hopeful that in-person alumni programming will return in the spring. Until then, we are planning virtual gatherings and presentations, which you can read about below. I wish you a fantastic fall and hope to see you online (and on campus) soon.

First-Year Course Examines History of Residential Schools
 

A new course at St. Michael’s will take a deep look at the history and legacy of residential schools in Canada. Christianity, Truth and Reconciliation is offered as a First-Year Foundations Seminar and is open to all first-year students in the Faculty of Arts & Science in the University of Toronto.

“My hope is that students come out of the course with the clear-eyed understanding of the way that European Christianity collaborated with the Canadian state to implement this program of cultural genocide—a clear-eyed view of the complicity in this project—but also a clear sense of other possibilities of relationship,” says course creator and Associate Professor Dr. Reid Locklin. Read the story online.

Catholic Culture in Canada & Women Religious in Canadian Higher Education
Monday, October 18 at 1:30-2:30 pm EDT, via Zoom
With historians Dr. Mark McGowan and Dr. Elizabeth Smyth
In partnership with the Loretto Sisters and the Sisters of St. Joseph

It was women’s religious communities that opened the college doors for female students more than 100 years ago, with the arrival of the Loretto Sisters and the Sisters of St. Joseph as professors and advocates for women’s higher education. This site celebrates, through archival materials and historical accounts, how these two religious communities changed the face of St. Michael’s.

In partnership with the Loretto Sisters and the Sisters of St. Joseph, join us for a conversation on Catholic Culture in Canada & Women Religious in Canadian Higher Education, with historians Dr. Mark McGowan and Dr. Elizabeth Smyth.
