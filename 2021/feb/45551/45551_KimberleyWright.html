“ The Pandemic Endgame: Where Do We Go From Here? ”
Living with uncertainty has been a defining feature of the COVID-19 pandemic. With the arrival of vaccines and expected seasonal declines in transmission, there is an end in sight. But uncertainty remains: the emergence of SARS-CoV-2 variants that are more transmissible or can elude our immune responses mean our actions over the next several months will be crucial. Ashleigh Tuite will discuss what we can learn from communicable disease epidemiology about the possible trajectory of the COVID-19 pandemic and what we might expect to see over the coming months and years.
Ashleigh Tuite, PhD MPH MSc, is an infectious disease epidemiologist and Assistant Professor at the Dalla Lana School of Public Health. She has contributed to the provincial and federal COVID-19 pandemic response and serves on the Ontario COVID-19 Modelling Consensus Table.
