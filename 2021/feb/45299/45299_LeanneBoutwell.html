This past year resulted in both hardships and progress for our community. We have remained connected and committed during these isolating times. We have also carried forward with important work within our public health, health care systems and at the School, all led capably by many of our alumni, students, faculty, staff, volunteers and other leaders.

As a community, we continue to rise during these unprecedented times, providing public health guidance, contributing to cutting-edge modelling and research, while taking important steps to confront and defend other issues of critical concern and public impact.

Preparing the next generation of public health and health care leaders is critical to our success and student support is more crucial than ever. As our students prepare to become influential leaders and promising innovators, they are training to continue to improve and create equitable, sustainable, and impactful public health and health care systems.

DLSPH and IHPME students provide much hope for the future. They have shown incredible feats of determination and ingenuity. There is no doubt that our society’s health is in good hands with our students and with alumni like you at the forefront.
