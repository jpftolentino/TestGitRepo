Career Assistance


We are here to help you as you consider career paths and options. As a new A&S graduate, you continue to have access to career counselling services for up to two years after graduation. You’re also invited to three career-focused workshops exclusively for the Class of 2021, with special tips for next steps after graduation:

How to Make Your Resume Tell a Story

July 12, 12–1 pm EDT

Learn strategies to create a more effective and captivating resume. In this interactive session, you will learn about the fundamentals of writing a compelling job-specific resume, including proper formatting tips, effective keyword integration and writing accomplishment statements.

Register

Navigating your Career in the Midst of COVID-19

July 14, 12–1pm EDT

In a special session led by The LinkedIn Guys, you’ll be prepared to adjust your interviewing strategy to make the most of video interviews; stand out with recruiters and hiring managers on LinkedIn, no matter how competitive the applicant pool; and chart your career course to the industries and firms poised to hire throughout the crisis and beyond — and much more.

Register

Navigating your Job Search during Pandemic

July 27, 12–1 pm EDT

The search for work is a dynamic, often complex process with multiple components. Many recent graduates struggle with how to get started. In this session, you will learn about approaches and resources to help you target your search and stay motivated. These strategies will support you to improve your personalized job search action plan.

Register

Wait, there’s more!


U of T offers a host of benefits exclusive to our amazing alumni, including:

  • A School of Continuing Studies (SCS) credit toward a course during the five years following your graduation. SCS offers more than 600 non-degree courses and 80 specialized certificate programs, such as project management, leadership, digital strategy, business communications, innovation, languages and more
  • Ten Thousand Coffees, an online tool for U of T students, recent grads and alumni to connect online over coffee (or tea)
  • Events, workshops and lectures in the GTA and around the world
  • Free massive online open courses (MOOCs) on a variety of topics in partnership with Coursera and EdX
  • Preferred-rate financial products and retail discounts
  • Various volunteer opportunities, from mentoring to serving on Governing Council (online until further notice)


And much more!
