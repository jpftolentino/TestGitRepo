GeoPlan – Department of Geography & Planning Newsletter 2020-21
 
 
 
MESSAGES FROM THE CHAIRS
 
Message from Chair Richard DiFrancesco

Welcome to the 2020-21 edition of GeoPlan. This has been a year and a half that will not soon be forgotten. On March 16, 2020, Business Officer Maria Wowk and I locked the doors to the department, thinking we would likely be working remotely for a month at most. At that time, all undergraduate and graduate courses were required to pivot to a virtual format, which was quite jarring given that most of our courses had not been designed to operate virtually. Labs and assignments which required collaborative, face-to-face work were in play, and in-person final exams were on the horizon. The extent of the disruption cannot be overstated.

Read more on the Department of Geography & Planning website.


Message from Graduate Chair Ron Buliung

I am incredibly grateful to our amazing graduate students, staff and faculty for their commitment to one another and their programs of study during an incredibly difficult year. Thanks also to our loved ones for their support throughout this challenging time. I would also like to take a moment to acknowledge the work of our graduate associate chairs, Professors Sharlene Mollett, Scott Prudham and Katharine Rankin (planning director), as well as staff who support our graduate programs — namely Jessica Finlayson, Yvonne Kenny, Maria Wowk and Parveen Malli. Professor Mollett will be moving into a new role in 2021-22 at the University of Toronto Scarborough as chair of the Department of Global Development Studies. Congratulations!

Read more on the Department of Geography & Planning website.

 
 
NEWS AND EVENTS
 

Welcome new faculty!
Over the last two years, we have welcomed Professor Harald Bathelt; Associate Professor Natalie Oswin; Assistant Professors Hulya Arik, Michelle Daigle, Carolyn DeLoyde, Heather Dorries, Rachel Goffe, Christopher Higgins, Zachary Hyde, Damian Maddalena, Sarah Peirce and Lindsay Stephens; and Adjunct Professors (2020-22) Zahra Ebrahim and Kofi Hope. Read more about their work and research.
Interview with Ian Burton
Known for his work on natural hazards and his advocacy for adaptation, Professor Emeritus Ian Burton — recipient of the 2021 Banco Bilbao Vizcaya Argentaria Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award in Climate Change — applies a geographic perspective to addressing climate change. In our interview, he discusses his research, his work with the U.N., the future of climate change and words of advice for geography students.
Columba Gonzalez-Duarte on butterfly and human migrations
Postdoctoral fellow Columba Gonzalez-Duarte studies the ecology of the monarch, the relationship between butterflies and people, and the butterfly’s affective and symbolic weight, resulting in research that breaks down borders between multiple disciplines and countries.
Recognizing and reflecting on anti-Black racism for anti-racist futures
Associate Graduate Chair and Professor Sharlene Mollett reflects on racial injustice and the urgency to create more equitable ways forward. She recognizes the diverse responses received from University of Toronto Scarborough students in submissions for the Department of Human Geography Anti-Black Racism Awards.
Re-imagining teaching during a global pandemic
Using ArcGIS StoryMaps, Assistant Professor Sarah Peirce gives an overview of physical geography at the department and shares innovative ways she and her colleagues taught the subject over the last year, including 360-degree photography and lab videos.
Health geography research across the U of T tri-campus
Faculty and students share their research in the field of health geography, including pertinent topics like food access, air pollution, social isolation, the disabling experiences of urban life and community and policy responses to health inequalities — many of which have been exacerbated in the time of COVID-19.
Geography & planning students support Toronto’s growing community land trust movement
Faculty and students respond to Toronto's housing crisis by supporting community initiatives and alternative housing solutions, including community land trusts — member-based non-profit organizations that obtain and manage land for community ownership and democratic control.
The planning program review: addressing racism in planning
This year the planning program undertook a curricular and program review with a specific focus on addressing anti-Black racism in the program and the planning discipline more broadly. Director Katharine Rankin reflects on the process and the collaborative efforts of faculty members, students and professional planners.
