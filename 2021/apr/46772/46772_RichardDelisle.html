The world lost a titan of science recently with the passing of former University of Toronto Professor Donald Alexander Stuart Fraser. A trailblazer in the field of statistics, he passed away December 21, 2020 at the age of 95.

On a personal note, I was honoured and privileged to know Don as a colleague for almost 20 years. His research was inspirational to all of us, and we continue to strive to live up to his high standards and exemplary work ethic.

We are now asking you to join us in continuing his remarkable legacy with a memorial fund that will support a new generation of young scholars. The Department of Statistical Sciences will match the first $15,000 in donations, dollar for dollar.

Don’s role in establishing U of T and Canada at the epicentre of the “Big Data” boom that dominates today’s world cannot be overstated. Statistics was not yet recognized as its own academic discipline when he started his career 70 years ago. Today, the department is one of the largest in the Faculty of Arts & Science, and U of T is recognized as one of the world leaders in this field of study.

"Don started working in a different era, but he played a large role in making Canada a place where people go to talk statistics because he had students who had students who had students, and it grew from there," Nancy Reid, a University Professor and Don’s wife, who published more than 50 research papers with her husband, told me recently.

"In some sense, he laid the foundation for our department to flourish. For a long time, we were a small department in the faculty, but Don gave us a strong base for our recent explosive growth."


After graduating with a bachelor of arts as a member of Trinity College in 1946 and earning his master of arts and PhD from Princeton University, Don returned to U of T as an assistant professor in mathematics in 1949 and stayed for most of his career.

Don was responsible, almost single-handedly, for establishing U of T’s international reputation as a leader in the foundations of the statistical sciences. He played a key role in the establishment of the University’s Department of Statistical Sciences, serving as its first chairman from 1977 to 1983 and building a broadly based research group.

Don’s stature attracted talent that helped U of T become a centre of excellence in statistics, which it remains to this day. We want to keep it that way.

With your support, the Donald A. S. Fraser Memorial Award Fund will help inspire and attract the best and brightest statisticians and data scientists of tomorrow.

Thank you for considering a gift to honour Don’s legacy and keep U of T at the forefront of this rapidly evolving field.




Sincerely,


Radu Craiu, PhD
Professor and Chair
Department of Statistical Sciences
University of Toronto
