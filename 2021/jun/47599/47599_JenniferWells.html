MESSAGE FROM THE CHAIR
 
 
Dear Alumni and Community Supporters,

If I had to come up with a tagline to summarize this edition of the Department of Political Science newsletter, it would be: Political science is a life sentence. I say that because this issue of Politics really emphasizes the full life course of our community. You will read about the ways in which our undergraduate students turned the challenge of online learning into new and creative ways to engage the study of politics. You will learn about how some of my colleagues are speaking to urgent questions of global significance, and you will be introduced to our newest faculty members, who will be joining us later this summer. This edition of the newsletter also features the department’s thriving mentorship program, which matches senior undergraduate students with alumni mentors, as well as several other graduates of the department whose careers have been informed by the study of political science and the professors they encountered here. Finally, this issue contains a profile of my remarkable emeritus colleague Bob Matthews and a tribute to the late José Nun.

To end on a personal note: I suppose I am more attentive than most to the dynamics of the academic life course since I am preparing to retire (gradually, and I hope gracefully!) from active duty. It has been my privilege to serve as Acting Chair of the Department of Political Science during this very challenging year — and for 40-odd years as a professor before that. Please keep in touch. We are just a phone call, email or tweet away.

With many thanks,

Rob Vipond
Professor and Acting Chair
