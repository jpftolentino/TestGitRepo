MESSAGE FROM THE CHAIR: PROFESSOR JANET POOLE

Dear friends of the Department of East Asian Studies,

Welcome to our first newsletter. We hope you will enjoy hearing the latest news about the Department of East Asian Studies. Over the past year, we have faced many challenges because of the pandemic, which forced us to move all of our teaching online with little advance notice. As all of our communications were switched into virtual modes, I personally missed the day-to-day camaraderie of working in the department’s office. At the same time, I have been humbled to see how much we have still been able to achieve. Dissertations have been written and defended, examinations completed, MA degrees obtained, and our specialists, majors, and minors have been launched into the world. In our first issue of this newsletter, we would like to share the stories of some of our students and their achievements. We also introduce you to our newest faculty member, Professor Nathan Vedal, who chose to join us despite only meeting us through Zoom.

Whether you are one of our alumni or simply interested in the work that we do, we would like to stay in touch with you. Our plan is to publish twice a year. Feel welcome to email me at eas.chair@utoronto.ca to share any news or thoughts on what you would like to see included in future editions of the newsletter. In the meantime, enjoy reading our stories and please stay well and healthy.

With best wishes,

Janet Poole
Department Chair

 
Stories from Our Community

Spotlight on Racial Justice

The Department of East Asian Studies is committed to fighting racial inequality and oppression in all forms. Please read our statement of support for the Black Lives Matter movement and a list of anti-Asian racism resources curated by EAS graduate student, Doyun Kim, with help from EAS Professors Michelle Cho and Lisa Yoneyama. Read more about Kim’s experience as a racialized student and his experience curating such an important resource for our community. If you’d like to learn more about our anti-racism efforts, please email Chair and Professor Janet Poole.

New Faculty Member: Nathan Vedal

Professor Nathan Vedal joined EAS this past July as our newest faculty member. We offer him a warm welcome to the EAS family! From his training as a musician to his unlikely journey to becoming a scholar of Chinese history and literature, we interviewed him to find out more about his research and what he’s most excited about for this new chapter at the University of Toronto. Hint: You might be seeing him at a concert venue or two.

Graduate Students in the News

EAS graduate students are second to none, as was proven through their stellar scholarship record this year. We profiled a few of them to talk about their research, the funding they received and how it will help them on their graduate journeys. We were happy to also chat with the first recipient of the Vincent Shen Memorial Scholarship in Chinese Thought and Culture, which was a scholarship created in memory of EAS Professor Vincent Shen.

Undergraduate Students in the News: Tif Fan

Tif Fan started off the new school year with a bang. The EAS and Political Science student was awarded the 2021-2022 Jackman Humanities Institute Undergraduate Fellowship. The honour allows scholars ranging from undergraduates to tenured faculty members to contribute to an annual theme (this year’s theme is “Pleasure”). Fan will be working on a research project titled A Panoply of Play: The Subversions and Conversions of Monkey King in Journey to the West under the supervision of EAS Professor Linda Rui Feng. Read more about Fan’s project and what they are most excited about for this unique undergraduate opportunity.

Alumni Spotlight:
Cailleah Scott-Grimes (BA 2011)

EAS & Visual Studies alumna Cailleah Scott-Grimes premiered her first short film, Between Us, at many film festivals this past summer, including Hot Docs and Inside Out. The film follows Kei, a young transgender man, and his queer Canadian partner as they navigate conflicting desires in rural Japan. Check out our interview with Scott-Grimes to learn more about how her time at EAS prepared her for the daunting task of filming in Japan, the bonds she was able to cultivate with LGBTQ2S+ communities, and her advice for new graduates.

 
Faculty Bookshelf

Professor Linda Rui Feng Publishes First Novel

Professor Linda Rui Feng’s debut novel, Swimming Back to Trout River, was published by Simon & Schuster earlier this year. It was recently longlisted for the Giller Prize, in addition to other literary award nominations.

"Told as a braided narrative of four intertwined lives, my novel traces the far-flung orbits of a family across two continents in the decades before and after the Cultural Revolution, and explores the themes of music, migration, and how the titanic forces of history precipitate in smaller, more recessive lives,” says Professor Feng.

Read more about Professor Feng as she discusses the novel and her work as an academic and fiction writer.

Professor Hsiao-wei Rupprecht Publishes Chinese Language Textbook

Professor Hsiao-wei Rupprecht recently co-authored a Chinese language textbook: Speaking Out: Issues and Controversies. The textbook presents 10 lessons that reflect major concerns in contemporary China and invites students to reflect upon the forces shaping modern Chinese society. Read more about the textbook and its aim to bridge gaps in current Chinese language learning.

Professors Kyoungrok Ko and Yujeong Choi Publish Interactive Korean Language Textbook

Professors Kyoungrok Ko and Yujeong Choi, along with other writers, authored a Korean language textbook, New Generation Korean: Beginner Level, including interactive features like audio files and a vocabulary app. The textbook is aimed at beginners at the secondary and post-secondary education levels, as well as learners studying Korean independently. In addition to audio files and a vocabulary app, the textbook offers full-colour images and engaging activities. Read our interview with Professor Ko about how the textbook is setting a new precedent for language acquisition and textbook production.

 
General Updates

Professor Johanna Liu Retires

Much-beloved Professor Johanna Liu retired this past summer. An expert in the field of aesthetics, philosophy and the Chinese arts, Professor Liu joined our department in 2001. Over the past two decades, Professor Liu has mentored numerous students towards their doctoral, masters and undergraduate degrees. Our community has benefited from her enthusiastic organization of workshops and conferences, which brought together scholars from all over the world for engaged and sustained discussion. Our daily lives in the department have been richer for her presence among us. We will dearly miss Professor Liu but are grateful for all her work and care for us in our departmental lives. To celebrate Professor Liu’s many achievements and to share precious memories with her, the department presented her with a memory book, produced by faculty and students. Our professors Yujeong Choi and Graham Sanders presented Professor Liu with the memory book this summer, together with an inspiring work of calligraphy! We wish you a long, peaceful and happy retirement, Professor Liu, thank you for working among us for so long.

Graduate Alumni Updates

Our graduate alumni have gone on to work at prestigious post-secondary institutions, international organizations and in government, non-profits and more. We are happy to congratulate our recent graduate alumni: Sara Osenton (PhD 2017) for beginning a tenure-track position as a Learning Specialist at Emily Carr University and Yanfei Li (PhD 2018), who is now the Coordinator of the Chinese Studies Program at Dalhousie University. To learn more about where our PhD students have landed after graduating, please visit the PhD Placements page on our website.
