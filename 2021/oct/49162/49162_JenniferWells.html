Help us complete the story of Old English

The Dictionary of Old English (DOE) is close to a new milestone in our quest to complete the historical description of the English lexicon.

We are now past the half-way mark to completing the DOE, and, by the end of 2022, we aim to finish and publish the first half of the letter "L", from "La "to "Le." (Remember — Q, V, and Z are not ordinarily used in Old English!)

With your generous support, our work translating and publishing entries for the DOE can continue and approximately 700 entries for "L" will soon be online and accessible to the public.

We are so close to reaching a new milestone — help us get there!

Founded in 1970 at U of T — Canada’s top research university and home of the world-renowned Centre for Medieval Studies — the completed DOE will form a lexicographic trilogy with the Middle English Dictionary and the Oxford English Dictionary.

As government funding for the humanities continues to shrink, the DOE is even more dependent on you.

Historical dictionaries require significant resources and sustained support. Some letters require more time than others to complete, and progress fluctuates depending on funding.

If you have supported the DOE in the past, we are deeply grateful for your generosity and hope you will continue to support us.

If you haven’t yet, we hope you will consider supporting us now.
